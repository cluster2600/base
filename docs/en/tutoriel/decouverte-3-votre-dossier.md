# Your folder, your first document

*⏱ ~12 min · module 3/3, Discovery track*

**You will**: have your own BASE space with one of your real documents inside it, and know that it is genuinely being read, proven by the ✅ below.
**You need**: modules 1-2 completed.
↻ **Recap**: without looking, what makes the assistant's behavior change? (editing its files)

1. Copy the `exemples/starter-perso` folder out of the repository (into your Documents), and
   rename it, for example `mon-assistant`. With the keyboard: right-click -> Copy -> Paste. Or
   ask your assistant: *"copy the starter-perso folder into my Documents"*.
2. Open THIS new folder in your tool (*File -> Open Folder*).
3. Drag one of your real documents into it (a procedure, a reference sheet). Safe formats: `.md`, `.txt`.
   If the format does not go through, paste the text into a new `.md` file.
4. Ask three things (the **interrogation protocol**):
   - a question that ONLY your document can answer (it must cite the document);
   - a question outside your document (it must say it does not know);
   - a request that should route to one of your tasks.

✅ **Check**: answer 1 cites your document; answer 2 admits it does not know instead of inventing; answer 3 lands on the right intent. The three together prove the import is real and that the assistant is not bluffing.

💡 **Why it worked**: a successful import is not "the file is there" but "the assistant uses it and stays honest about its limits". The interrogation protocol tests exactly that, with no tooling at all.

🔁 **At home**: which of your documents deserves to be the first real content in your assistant?

→ **And now**: you have completed the Discovery tour. To build for real (processes, evaluation, fieldwork), move on to the [Practitioner track](praticien-1-anatomie.md).

🆘 **Common breakdowns**: *It invents an answer to question 2*: that is the risk of the *consigne* tier; deterministic routing and a good use_when reduce it (Practitioner track). *It does not see your document*: check that it is really INSIDE the open folder, not somewhere else.

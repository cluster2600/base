## What BASE takes seriously

BASE starts from a few sober observations about working with AI: no hype, no doom, only what holds up to scrutiny.

<CardGrid>
  <Card title="Generating is not verifying.">
  A model's core produces probable completions; nothing within it tests them against reality. Verification is therefore external: a fact, a calculation, a tool can check; but what engages your intentions, your context, your values, you alone can judge.
  </Card>
  <Card title="What you don't say, AI invents.">
  Natural language is under-specified: it leaves gaps. The model fills them with the most probable regularities of its training, not with your rules. What is left implicit is not merely mishandled: handed to agents, it gets executed.
  </Card>
  <Card title="An instruction is not a guarantee.">
  An instruction given to the model stays probabilistic: it may follow it, or not. A guarantee, strictly speaking, comes from elsewhere: a deterministic check, external to the model, code that enforces it. BASE never dresses one as the other.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Autonomy without dialogue is an illusion.">
  A system with no external feedback optimizes its internal coherence, not its correctness: it has no way to know when it is wrong. Without a check, human or mechanism, autonomy fails where judgment is needed, whatever the power of the model.
  </Card>
  <Card title="What you don't understand, you won't maintain.">
  An assistant you don't understand becomes impossible to fix the day the context shifts, and it always does. What cannot be reread cannot be maintained.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Sovereignty is not just about your servers.">
  Where your data lives matters, of course. But an AI can run on your own servers and still be foreign in how it works. The sovereignty most often forgotten is elsewhere: mastering how your knowledge is written and steered. Keep it in files you can read, correct and take with you.
  </Card>
  <Card title="What advances fastest is efficiency, not size.">
  The dominant story measures AI by the size of its models and its data centers. Yet what advances fastest is efficiency: for a given capability, the compute required falls year after year, faster than hardware improves. For most everyday knowledge work (conversing, drafting, reformulating, following a framed process), a good local machine and a free model already suffice, and that frontier keeps receding; the giant infrastructures serve mainly a different kind of AI. The real leverage is not the power you rent, but how you structure the work with these models.
  </Card>
  <Card title="A model is a computation, not a civilization.">
  The world's richness comes not from one intelligence, but from the diversity of billions of minds: each explores a corner no one else would have reached, and that is often where, by chance, a discovery is born. A model, however powerful, is a single way of seeing; a thousand copies are a thousand times the same. One more reason to keep the human, and that diversity, at the heart of the work.
  </Card>
</CardGrid>

**Reliability is not bought. It is built.**
